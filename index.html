What is Soccer

Soccer is a sports event which is played by two teams which compete against each other every team needs to have at least 11 players which one of them must be the goalkeeper. So, it is not allowed for the players on the field except the goalkeeper to use their hands during the game, the goalkeeper can use their hands only in the specified areas there are some other rules which I will mention in the rules of soccer part. Soccer is considered a more popular sport in Europe compared to the US. The major soccer tournaments are called the champions league, the National European league, and the World Cup so these are the major tournaments of soccer. Also, soccer is usually played in stadiums where there are a lot of fans supporting their teams

About Soccer

So, soccer is a sport that seems easy to play but in fact, it is a difficult sport. People give a lot of effort to be a professional soccer player because it requires a lot of factors to be combined such as skills, determination, mentality, and ambition

Rules of Soccer

So, there are some certain rules of Soccer. The first rule is none of the players except the goalkeepers can use their hands during the game. So, soccer is a sport which is played by using the foot. That's why except for the goalkeeper's other players can not use their hands. Players can use their hands only if they are using a throw-in. In addition, goalkeepers can not use their hands in every area of the pitch the only area that they can use their hands is the specified area for them. The second rule is players have to leave the pitch if they receive a red card if they refuse to leave the pitch it is an action against the rules. The third rule is the duration of the games is 90 minutes which is divided into two halves. So, every half is 45 minutes in total without the extra time. Another rule is in each game every team can make a maximum of 3 substitutions during the game, this rule has been modified in some leagues but normally in most leagues, every team can make 3 substitutions.
